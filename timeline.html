Early Nintendo: 1889-1981

September 23rd 1889

Nintendo Koppa was founded by Fusajiro Yamauchi in Kyoto, Japan. They started out as a manufacturer of hanafuda (Japanese playing cards)

1929

Yamauchi retires from Nintendo Koppa without a son, so adopts his son-in-law to take over the business according to Japanese tradition

1933

The company is renamed Yamauchi Nintendo & Company

1950

Hiroshi Yamauchi becomes the company’s third president

1951

The name of the company is changed to Nintendo Playing Card Co. Ltd.

1953

Nintendo becomes the first company in Japan to produce playing cards made of plastic

1963

The company is renamed to Nintendo by Yamauchi. The company begins to expand its business, including Instant Rice and Vacuum Cleaners, all of which eventually fail. During this time, it is also reported that Nintendo opened its own Love Hotel

1966

Gunpei Yokoi, a conveyor belt repair main for Nintendo, creates the Ultra Machine toy, an “extending arm-shaped toy” (Wikipedia) and is a massive success for the company. Yokoi would go on to develop the Ten Billion Barrel puzzle, and a Love Tester

1970

Nintendo releases the first solar-powered light gun named the Nintendo Beam Gun, the first of its kind for home use

1973

The company developed a laser clay shooting system using the same technology from there Beam Gun toys for some arcade machines, which were quite successful, leading Nintendo down a new path

1974

Nintendo developed an image-projection system and employed the 16mm film projector in amusement arcades. The company began exporting these arcade machines to America and Europe. Nintendo also releases its first video arcade machine EVR Race

1980

Nintendo begins testing the handheld video game market with its fist product from the Game & Watch line. These would continue to be released until 1991, with around 60 different variants being made. Special editions are still released to this day.

1981

Shigeru Miyamoto designs Donkey Kong for the arcade, quickly becoming one of the most successful arcade games ever released. This then lead to Nintendo looking more at the Home gaming market
